Chiriya Urri — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 12 July 2026

Chiriya Urri is a casual game available on the web, Android, and iOS. This policy describes what information the game handles. The short version: we do not collect, store, or sell your personal data. We run no servers and no analytics.

What stays on your device

The player name you type, your recent room codes, and (for a room's host) the room's game state are saved only in your browser's or app's local storage on your own device, so a refresh can restore your game. They are never sent to us — we have no server to send them to. You can clear them at any time by clearing the app's/site's data.

Online multiplayer

Online rooms connect players directly to each other (peer-to-peer, WebRTC). Your chosen display name and your answers are shared with the other players in your room — that is the game. To establish the connection, a signalling service (the public PeerJS broker) briefly relays connection metadata, and as with any peer-to-peer connection, devices in the same room can technically observe each other's IP addresses. Game traffic itself flows between players, not through us.

Advertising

The current version of the game shows no ads.

A future update of the mobile apps may show ads served by Google AdMob. If and when that ships: AdMob (Google) may process device identifiers (such as the advertising ID) and coarse usage data to serve and measure ads; where required (for example in the EEA/UK), you will be shown a consent choice before any ad loads, and you can revisit that choice from the game's menu ("Privacy options"); on iOS, the App Tracking Transparency prompt lets you decline tracking, in which case ads are still shown but are not personalized. Google's own practices are described at policies.google.com/technologies/ads. This section becomes effective only when an app version containing ads is released, and this policy's date will be updated then.

Children

The game is suitable for general audiences, requires no account, and collects no personal data from anyone, including children.
